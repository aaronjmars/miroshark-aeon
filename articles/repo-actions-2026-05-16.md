## Ecosystem Context

Two PRs shipped yesterday that expand MiroShark's integration footprint in different directions: PR #83 gave the webhook notification layer platform-native formatting (Discord embed + Slack Block Kit), and PR #84 added OriginTrail DKG on-chain citation — making every finished sim a provenance artifact on the DKG. Taken together, they move MiroShark from "a simulation tool with a share page" toward "a trust layer for AI-driven decision-making that broadcasts completion events across multiple channels and registers them on-chain."

Three gaps become visible from this new baseline:

**The unfurl gap.** @revaultdrops is live, Discord and Slack notifications are live, and the share card (PR #42) handles Open Graph. But there's a fourth paste context that still shows a bare URL: writing platforms — Notion, Ghost, Substack, WordPress. All four implement the oEmbed spec. A single `GET /oembed?url=` route + a `<link rel="alternate">` discovery tag turns every `/share/<id>` paste in those platforms into an automatically-unfurled sim card — scenario title, belief thumbnail, iframe embed — without any user action.

**The on-chain audience gap.** $MIROSHARK lives on Base. The Base-native social layer is Farcaster/Warpcast. When a token holder shares a MiroShark sim in a Farcaster cast today, the cast shows a blank link card. Farcaster Frame v2 meta tags — a handful of `<meta property="fc:frame:*">` tags in the share page `<head>` — render the belief chart SVG (PR #85) as the preview image with a "View Simulation" action button. This is the lowest-friction on-chain distribution play available: no backend, no deps, 30 minutes of HTML, and every Farcaster cast containing a MiroShark share URL becomes an interactive Frame card.

**The notification quadrant gap.** MiroShark now sends completion events to webhook, Discord, and Slack. Email is absent — but email is the one channel that requires no platform account, no OAuth, and no webhook URL. An operator running MiroShark for a research team or hedge fund can reach their mailing list via `smtplib` (stdlib) with two env vars. The fire-and-forget daemon-thread dispatch and `(sim_id, status)` dedup pattern are already proven in `discord_notify.py` — email is a copy with `smtplib.SMTP` substituted for `urllib.request`.

Two analytical surfaces round out the batch:

**The trajectory insight gap.** The trajectory chart SVG (PR #85) shows the full belief curve visually; `trajectory.csv` (PR #66) gives the raw round-by-round table. Neither answers "which round did the bullish stance peak?" — an analyst has to parse the CSV themselves. `GET /api/simulation/<id>/peak-round` returns bullish/neutral/bearish peaks plus the most volatile round (largest total stance swing) in one call. It completes the analytical quadrant alongside reproduce.json (citation), trajectory.csv (raw data), and chart.svg (visual).

**The operator identity gap.** MiroShark's gallery (`/explore`) shows all public sims globally but has no per-operator view. An operator who has run 30 published simulations can't share "all my work" in a single link. `/profile/<operator_name>` is a per-operator gallery page with a summary card (total sims, consensus distribution, average quality, total views). Operators build a public research identity; researchers can follow a specific operator's work. Makes MiroShark a community platform, not just a tool.

### 1. oEmbed Endpoint

**Type:** Integration
**Effort:** Small (hours)
**Impact:** Every paste of a `/share/<id>` URL into Notion, Ghost, Substack, or WordPress auto-expands into a MiroShark sim card — scenario title, share-card thumbnail, and an iframe embed — without the user copying any embed code. The oEmbed spec is natively implemented by all four platforms; the missing piece is a `<link rel="alternate" type="application/json+oembed">` discovery tag on the share page and a `GET /oembed?url=` endpoint returning oEmbed JSON. Once live, every analyst citing a MiroShark sim in their Notion notes or Substack article gets an automatic embedded preview. Re-eligible from May 8 batch (unbuilt). Closes the platform-distribution gap that the share card (PR #42) and EmbedDialog address for manual embeds — this version is automatic on paste.

**How:**
1. Add `backend/app/services/oembed_service.py` (~80 LoC, pure stdlib `re` + `urllib.parse` + `json`). `parse_sim_id_from_url(url: str) -> str | None`: regex `r'/share/([a-zA-Z0-9_-]{4,64})'` against the URL; returns `None` for non-MiroShark URLs or invalid IDs. `build_oembed_response(sim_state, sim_id, base_url) -> dict`: type `"rich"`, `provider_name: "MiroShark"`, `provider_url: base_url`, `title: sim_state["scenario_title"][:100]`, `thumbnail_url: f"{base_url}/api/simulation/{sim_id}/share-card.png"`, `thumbnail_width: 1200`, `thumbnail_height: 630`, `width: 800`, `height: 500`, `html: f'<iframe src="{base_url}/embed/{sim_id}" width="800" height="500" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>'`. Add `GET /oembed` route (no auth): reads `url` query param, validates the domain matches the MiroShark base URL (reject foreign domains — return 404), parses `sim_id`, loads sim state (publish-gated — 404 for unpublished sims), returns `Content-Type: application/json+oembed`. Add 8 offline unit tests in `test_unit_oembed.py`: valid share URL extracts sim_id, foreign domain rejected (404), unpublished sim 404, response type is `"rich"`, html contains `<iframe>`, thumbnail_url contains `share-card.png`, title capped at 100 chars, provider_name is `"MiroShark"`.

2. In the share page `<head>` — identify how the existing Open Graph tags (`<meta property="og:title">` etc.) are injected (server-side template, Vue `useHead`, or `index.html` dynamic population) and follow the same pattern to inject: `<link rel="alternate" type="application/json+oembed" href="{base_url}/oembed?url={url_encoded_share_url}" title="{scenario_title_first_60_chars}">`. Also add `<link rel="alternate" type="text/xml+oembed" href="...">`  pointing to the same endpoint (Notion checks for the XML variant — returning JSON from the XML type is spec-compliant; no second endpoint needed). Inject only for published sims, consistent with the OG tag gating. Update `frontend/src/api/simulation.js` with a `getOEmbedUrl(simId, baseUrl)` helper.

3. Add `GET /oembed` to `openapi.yaml` under Publish & Embed with an `OEmbedResponse` schema. Add "oEmbed Endpoint" to `docs/FEATURES.md` under Publish & Embed with a one-line compatibility note: "Auto-unfurl in Notion, Ghost, Substack, WordPress." Add `docs/OEMBED.md` (brief): what oEmbed is, how to test it (paste a share URL into Notion), and a curl example. Zero new deps.

### 2. Farcaster Frame for Share Page

**Type:** Growth
**Effort:** Small (hours)
**Impact:** $MIROSHARK lives on Base. The Base-native social network is Farcaster/Warpcast, where the token's audience is concentrated. When a researcher or token holder shares a `/share/<id>` URL in a Farcaster cast today, the cast shows a blank link card. Farcaster Frame v2 meta tags (`fc:frame`, `fc:frame:image`, `fc:frame:button:1`) in the share page `<head>` render the belief chart SVG (PR #85, available once merged) as the preview image with a "View Simulation" action button inside Warpcast and every Frame-aware Farcaster client — zero backend, zero new npm deps, pure HTML meta tags. Distribution: every Farcaster cast containing a MiroShark share URL becomes an interactive sim card directly in the feed of the Base-chain crypto audience that holds $MIROSHARK. Not previously suggested.

**How:**
1. In the share page component (the Vue route that serves `/share/:simId`, or the server-side template — identify how the existing `<meta property="og:image">` is injected and follow that same pattern), add Farcaster Frame v2 tags when the sim is published and has a non-empty trajectory (trajectory data is required for `chart.svg`). Tags to inject: `<meta property="fc:frame" content="next">`, `<meta property="fc:frame:image" content="{base_url}/api/simulation/{sim_id}/chart.svg">`, `<meta property="fc:frame:image:aspect_ratio" content="2:1">` (800×400 viewBox → 2:1), `<meta property="fc:frame:button:1" content="View Simulation →">`, `<meta property="fc:frame:button:1:action" content="link">`, `<meta property="fc:frame:button:1:target" content="{base_url}/share/{sim_id}">`. These tags are silently ignored by non-Farcaster renderers — adding them has no downside for other consumers. Check whether SSR is in play; if the share page is a fully client-side SPA, note that Farcaster Frames require the meta tags to be present in the initial HTML response (Farcaster fetches the URL server-side). If the share page is SPA-only, flag this in the PR — a lightweight server-side route for `/share/<id>` that returns pre-populated meta tags (already needed for OG unfurl) would serve both purposes.

2. Add `GET /api/simulation/<id>/frame-metadata` (publish-gated; no auth) → `{frame_version: "next", image_url: str, image_aspect_ratio: "2:1", buttons: [{label: "View Simulation →", action: "link", target: str}], sim_title: str, has_trajectory: bool}`. Not strictly required by the Farcaster spec (which reads meta tags from HTML), but lets the frontend build tags dynamically without hardcoding the base URL and allows future interactive Frame features (e.g., "Run new sim" button) to be added via backend config rather than HTML redeployment. `has_trajectory: false` suppresses Frame tag injection for sims with no trajectory data (a blank SVG would be a poor Frame image). Add 6 offline unit tests in `test_unit_frame_metadata.py`: published sim returns 200, unpublished returns 403, `image_url` contains `chart.svg`, button action is `"link"`, `frame_version` is `"next"`, `sim_title` truncated to 80 chars.

3. Add `getFrameMetadata(simId)` to `frontend/src/api/simulation.js`. In `EmbedDialog.vue`, add a "🟣 Farcaster Frame" section (publish-gated; additionally gated on `has_trajectory: true`). Layout: brief explanation ("This simulation is Farcaster Frame-ready — paste the share link in any Farcaster client to embed it as an interactive card."), a "Copy share URL" button (same share URL, just reusing the existing copy action), and a Warpcast compose link: `https://warpcast.com/~/compose?embeds[]={encoded_share_url}` — opens the Warpcast composer pre-filled with the share URL so the operator can see the Frame preview before casting. Add "Farcaster Frame" to `docs/FEATURES.md` under Publish & Embed. Add to README.md Features table. Zero new deps.

### 3. Email Completion Notifications

**Type:** Integration
**Effort:** Small (hours)
**Impact:** MiroShark completion events now reach webhook, Discord, and Slack. Email is absent — but email is the one channel that requires no platform account, no OAuth, and no webhook URL. An operator running MiroShark for a research team, a hedge fund back-office, or a solo analyst can route sim completions to a mailing list (or their own inbox) with two env vars (`SMTP_HOST`, `SMTP_TO`). The email body mirrors the Slack Block Kit structure — scenario title, belief percentages, quality badge, round count, share URL — in plain text + minimal HTML via `email.mime` (stdlib). Same fire-and-forget daemon-thread dispatch and `(sim_id, status)` dedup pattern as `discord_notify.py` and `slack_notify.py`. Zero new deps — `smtplib` + `email.mime.multipart` + `email.mime.text` are stdlib. Completes the four-channel notification quadrant. Not previously suggested.

**How:**
1. Add `backend/app/services/email_notify.py` (~150 LoC, pure stdlib `smtplib` + `email.mime.multipart` + `email.mime.text` + `ssl` + `os` + `threading`). Config reads: `SMTP_HOST` (required; if blank, module is a no-op), `SMTP_PORT` (default `587`), `SMTP_USER`, `SMTP_PASSWORD`, `SMTP_FROM`, `SMTP_TO` (comma-separated recipients). `build_email_message(sim_state, sim_id, base_url) -> MIMEMultipart`: `multipart/alternative` with a plain-text part and an HTML part. Plain text: scenario title (first 100 chars), three belief lines (`Bullish: {pct}%`, `Neutral: {pct}%`, `Bearish: {pct}%`), quality health, round count, agent count, share URL. HTML: `<html><body>` with scenario title as `<h2>`, belief rows as `<p>` with inline color spans (`#22c55e` bullish / `#6b7280` neutral / `#ef4444` bearish), quality badge, "View simulation →" `<a href>`. Subject line: `[MiroShark] {consensus_direction}: {scenario_title[:60]}`. `send_email_notification(message, to_addrs, smtp_config) -> bool`: `smtplib.SMTP(host, port)`, `starttls(context=ssl.create_default_context())`, `login(user, password)` only when both `SMTP_USER` and `SMTP_PASSWORD` are non-empty (unauthenticated relay support), `sendmail(from, to_addrs, message.as_string())`. Returns `True` on success, `False` on any exception — never raises, logs to stdout. `notify_if_configured(sim_state, sim_id, base_url)`: reads `SMTP_HOST`; if blank, returns immediately. Dedup: module-level `_sent = set()`, keyed by `(sim_id, status)`. Dispatches in `threading.Thread(daemon=True)`. Add 10 offline unit tests in `test_unit_email_notify.py`: `SMTP_HOST` unset → 0 SMTP calls (mock `smtplib.SMTP`), `SMTP_TO` with 3 addresses → `sendmail` called with list of 3, subject contains scenario title, plain-text part present, HTML part present, bullish pct appears in HTML, share URL in plain text, dispatch returns `True` on success, SMTP exception returns `False` (no raise), `(sim_id, status)` dedup — second call no-ops.

2. In `simulation_runner.py`, add `email_notify.notify_if_configured(sim_state, sim_id, base_url)` at the same three dispatch sites as `discord_notify` and `slack_notify` (completed, failed, `simulation_end` event). Order: generic webhook → Discord → Slack → Email — each independently fire-and-forget, each no-op if its env var is absent. Add `email_configured: bool` to `GET /api/config/notifications` (check `SMTP_HOST` non-empty). Add `email_configured` to the `NotificationsConfig` schema in `openapi.yaml`. Update `EmbedDialog.vue`'s notifications callout to show four chips: Webhook / Discord / Slack / Email. Add the six `SMTP_*` env vars to `.env.example` with inline comments: include a Gmail App Password note and a note that `SMTP_USER`/`SMTP_PASSWORD` may be omitted for unauthenticated SMTP relays.

3. Add email setup instructions to `docs/NOTIFICATIONS.md`: Gmail App Password path (App Passwords → generate → paste into `SMTP_PASSWORD`), self-hosted relay path (`SMTP_USER`/`SMTP_PASSWORD` both blank, `SMTP_PORT=25`), and a `smtplib` test snippet for verifying SMTP connectivity. Add "Email Notifications" row to `docs/FEATURES.md` under Notifications. Zero new deps.

### 4. Peak-Round Belief Analytics

**Type:** Feature
**Effort:** Small (hours)
**Impact:** The trajectory chart SVG (PR #85) shows the full belief curve visually; `trajectory.csv` (PR #66) gives the raw round-by-round table. Neither answers "which round did the bullish stance peak?" or "which round had the biggest belief swing?" without parsing the CSV. `GET /api/simulation/<id>/peak-round` returns `{bullish: {round, pct}, neutral: {round, pct}, bearish: {round, pct}, most_volatile_round: int, max_swing_pct: float, total_rounds: int}` — where `most_volatile_round` is the round with the largest Σ|Δstance| across all three stances, computed in one O(n) pass over trajectory.json. Re-eligible from May 8 batch (unbuilt). Gives quant operators a single API call to identify inflection points without loading the full CSV or interpreting the SVG. Complements reproduce.json (citation), trajectory.csv (raw data), and chart.svg (visual) with a machine-readable analytical summary.

**How:**
1. Add `backend/app/services/peak_round.py` (~120 LoC, pure stdlib `json` + `os`). `load_trajectory_rounds(sim_dir) -> list[dict]`: reads `trajectory.json` (same source as `trajectory_export.build_rows`), returns per-round `{round: int, bullish_pct: float, neutral_pct: float, bearish_pct: float}` dicts; returns `[]` on missing or corrupt file. `compute_peak_rounds(rounds: list[dict]) -> dict | None`: returns `None` for empty input. Single O(n) pass: tracks per-stance `(max_pct, round_num)` and per-round `total_delta = |Δbullish| + |Δneutral| + |Δbearish|` (comparing to previous round; round 0 has delta 0). Returns `{bullish: {round: int, pct: float}, neutral: {round: int, pct: float}, bearish: {round: int, pct: float}, most_volatile_round: int, max_swing_pct: float, total_rounds: int}` — all pct values rounded to 2 decimal places. `build_peak_round_payload(sim_dir, sim_id) -> dict | None`. Add `GET /api/simulation/<id>/peak-round` route (publish-gated; 404 `{"error": "no trajectory data yet"}` when `compute_peak_rounds` returns `None`). Increment `surface_stats` with key `peak_round`; extend `SURFACE_KEYS` frozenset. Add `PeakRoundResponse` to `openapi.yaml` under Analytics. Add 10 offline unit tests in `test_unit_peak_round.py`: single-round trajectory → all peaks at round 1, multi-round → bullish peak at correct round, empty trajectory → None, `most_volatile_round` correct on known delta sequence, `max_swing_pct` rounds to 2 decimal places, route 200 on published, route 403 on unpublished, route 404 on empty trajectory, `total_rounds` matches input length, `surface_stats` increment called.

2. Add `getPeakRound(simId)` to `frontend/src/api/simulation.js`. In `EmbedDialog.vue`, add a "📊 Peak beliefs" section (publish-gated; gated on trajectory data — show only when peak-round endpoint returns 200). Layout: four-field compact grid — `Bullish peak: {pct}% at round {round}`, `Bearish peak: {pct}% at round {round}`, `Most volatile round: {round} (±{max_swing_pct}% swing)`, `Total rounds: {n}`. Add "Copy peak-round JSON URL" button (same pattern as other copy-URL actions).

3. Add `GET /api/simulation/<id>/peak-round` to `docs/API.md` under Analytics with a definition of `most_volatile_round` (round with maximum total absolute stance change across all three stances). Add "Peak-Round Analytics" to `docs/FEATURES.md` under Data Export & Analysis. Extend `SimulationSurfaceStats` schema in `openapi.yaml` with `peak_round: {type: integer, minimum: 0}`. Zero new deps.

## Selection Rationale

This batch runs one layer above the distribution-surface arc that closed with PR #82 (21 PRs, #57–#82) — it's about reach, identity, and analytical completeness.

- **oEmbed Endpoint** (#1) — The auto-unfurl equivalent of the share card. Notion/Ghost/Substack all implement oEmbed; the spec is simple; the missing piece is two lines of HTML and one route. Every analyst citing a sim in a writing platform gets a preview embed on paste, without touching EmbedDialog.
- **Farcaster Frame** (#2) — Zero backend required; pure meta tags. But the distribution value is disproportionate: the Base-chain crypto audience that holds $MIROSHARK is on Farcaster. A sim posted in a Farcaster cast today is a blank link; with Frame tags it's an interactive belief-chart card. 30 minutes of work, direct reach to the highest-value attention pool.
- **Email Notifications** (#3) — The last missing notification channel. Universal (no platform required), stdlib (`smtplib`), and a clean copy of the existing discord/slack notifier pattern. Closes the four-channel quadrant.
- **Peak-Round Analytics** (#4) — The analytical counterpart to trajectory.csv and chart.svg. Quant operators want a machine-readable inflection point without parsing 100 rounds of CSV. One O(n) scan, one JSON endpoint.
- **Operator Profile** (#5) — The community layer. MiroShark has 1,164 stars and growing. A per-operator gallery page turns individual operators into research identities and gives researchers a discovery path ("all of @user's published simulations"). Medium effort but the network-effect payoff scales with star count.
