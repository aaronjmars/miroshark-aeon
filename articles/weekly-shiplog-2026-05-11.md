## What Shipped

### The Surface-Network Move — From Many Surfaces to a Loop That Closes

The throughline lands across five days and seven PRs. The eight surfaces that shipped through May 3 were each independent: a viewer landed on `/share/<id>`, or fetched `transcript.md`, or pulled the trajectory CSV, and that was the entire interaction. This week converted that fan of isolated endpoints into a network that observes itself.

**PR #72 Tweet Thread Export** (May 6) is the sixth share format and the first one whose primary consumer is the operator's *own* posting flow rather than a downstream subscriber. `GET /api/simulation/<id>/thread.txt` and `.json` walk the per-round series via the same `±0.2` stance threshold every other surface uses, emit one tweet per dominant-stance crossing, and cap at 15 tweets with a "N more flips" bridge tweet on truncation. The hysteresis is the load-bearing detail — without the ≥0.2pp lead requirement, every noisy 49/51 round would generate a flip-flop tweet. With it, only meaningful belief crossings become tweets. The EmbedDialog gets a 🧵 panel with per-tweet copy buttons and char counters; paste-to-X-compose is one click.

**PR #73 Webhook Delivery Log + Manual Retry** and **PR #74 Surface Usage Analytics** (May 7, **back-to-back at 12:11 and 12:25 UTC**) closed the operator-observability loop in a single session. PR #73 instruments the *outbound* edge (did our completion webhook actually reach Slack/Discord/n8n, what status, how long?) with an atomic-write `webhook-log.jsonl` capped at 50 lines per sim, URL-masked before any disk write, an admin-token-gated `GET .../webhook-log` and `POST .../webhook-retry` pair with a 5-second per-sim cooldown to prevent admin-token amplification attacks, and 32-thread barrier tests pinning the concurrency lock. PR #74 instruments the *inbound* edge with a `surface-stats.json` counter per sim that increments inside every `_serve_X` handler — share card, replay GIF, transcript MD/JSON, trajectory CSV/JSONL, thread TXT/JSON, watch page, feed atom/rss — frozen `SURFACE_KEYS` set locks the schema to 11 surfaces. Both PRs share the same architectural shape: pure-stdlib backend, atomic tempfile + `os.replace`, fire-and-forget at the call site, drift-tested OpenAPI updates, EmbedDialog panels with a Refresh button, and bilingual docs. After May 7, an operator running MiroShark for a fund or research group has a complete operator-facing feedback loop over what the platform sent out and what its audience pulled in.

**PR #75 Reproducibility Config Export** (May 8) is the citation primitive. `GET /api/simulation/<id>/reproduce.json` returns a v1-schema JSON document carrying every parameter another operator needs to re-run the same simulation — scenario, agent count, total rounds, platform toggles, time-config knobs, director events, and lineage (original / fork / counterfactual + parent ID + 140-char preview). Pretty-printed with `sort_keys=True + indent=2` so identical exports of a finished sim are bytewise-identical and the file's SHA-256 becomes a stable citation key for paper appendices. The schema-version pin (`SCHEMA_VERSION = "1"`) plus the `REQUIRED_KEYS` frozenset means a downstream parser can validate cheaply. Six of the prior surfaces made a finished sim *citable*; this one makes it *reproducible*.

**PR #76 Simulation Lineage Navigator** (May 9, merged 21:02 UTC) closes the navigation gap PR #75 left behind. The `parent_simulation_id` pointer was one-directional — a child sim knew its parent, but the parent had no view into its children. `GET /api/simulation/<id>/lineage` adds the reverse pointer: a `find_children()` reverse-pointer scan across the public corpus, public-only filter (private forks don't leak into a tweeted parent's lineage view — the privacy primitive the design rationale calls out explicitly), oldest-first sort, capped at 50 with an honest uncapped `total_children` count. The EmbedDialog gets a 🌳 Lineage panel that renders the parent + every public child as click-through rows tagged 🪐 Forked or 🔀 Counterfactual, with counterfactual rows surfacing trigger round + label inline ("At round 12 (ceo_resigns) · scenario preview…") so three branches off the same parent read as discrete narrative events instead of "slightly different scenarios."

**PR #77 + PR #78** (May 10, merged 22:39 and 22:41 UTC) close the loop. #77 extends PR #74's per-share-surface counters to also count `reproduce.json` and `lineage` endpoint hits, treating the citation primitive and the navigation primitive as first-class surfaces. #78 then adds `?sort=trending` to `/explore` that ranks public sims by cumulative `_serves_total` across all surfaces — the first feedback loop where what readers *pull* from the substrate decides what the substrate *shows them*. Eight isolated endpoints became a self-reinforcing observability → distribution → discovery loop, and the loop closed in a single Sunday-evening commit pair.

### The Agent-Side Self-Corrections (Four PRs in Four Days)

While MiroShark accreted surfaces, the harness running it caught and fixed four of its own latent failure modes. **PR #29 (May 6) replaced the project-lens skill's mathematically impossible "no repeat in 14 days" rule** (8 angle categories, daily cadence — unsatisfiable past day 8) with a least-recently-used rotation plus a soft 6-day floor, and added an explicit anti-pattern: "Do not invent your own justification narrative around a violated 14-day window." **PR #30 (May 6) added a daily volume-trend dimension to token-report** alongside the existing price track — and today's $MIROSHARK ATH session immediately exercised it (+34.8% 24h volume, the kind of signal that vanished from prior days' reports). **PR #31 (May 7) tightened heartbeat's skill-ran detection** from a case-insensitive substring scan of the full daily log to a case-insensitive grep against `^## ` header lines only — the old rule would falsely report the `feature` skill as having run on a day it failed because body text like "added a feature" anywhere in the log would substring-match. **PR #33 (May 10) capped MEMORY.md row sizes** after the index ballooned to 81 KB / 33K+ tokens (over Claude's Read-tool 25K-token limit), locking every skill out of its own context-loading step. The fix is two-pronged: tighten `memory-flush` to enforce per-row caps every Sun/Wed (Skills Built ≤280, Recent Articles ≤220, Recent Digests ≤180), and condense every existing row in place. File shrunk from 81 KB to ~7.7 KB; the read path is restored.

## Fixes & Improvements

- **PR #73 follow-up commit:** the `_LOG_WRITE_LOCK` (a module-level `threading.Lock` serializing the read-modify-rename window) and the 5-second per-sim retry cooldown were not in the initial commit — the author audited their own feature for racier failure modes and patched the gaps before merge. The 32-thread barrier test (`test_concurrent_appends_do_not_drop_entries`) is the durable pin.
- **PR #74 cache caveat in the UI:** the Distribution panel surfaces explicitly says counters increment per-origin hit and bypass the existing CDN/browser caches (1h share-card, 5min feed, 60s watch_page) — sets the right mental model in the surface itself, not in a buried doc.
- **PR #75 lineage-kind discriminator** (`_kind_for(state, cf)`) mirrors the same lineage-kind values PR #76 then reads back the next day — two surfaces over the same on-disk artifact never disagree on what counts as "fork" vs "counterfactual."
- **Stray `.aeon-tmp-verify-trending.py` scratch file** committed by the `feature` skill on May 10 alongside PR #78 — a 58-line transient verifier that should be `.gitignore`'d or cleaned up; flagged in yesterday's push-recap.

## Momentum Check

The shape of the work changed: PR #71 and #72 were still surface additions in the prior week's mold (one folder, one new format), but #73 onward shifted into a different mode — every PR after May 7 either *measured* the prior surfaces (#74), made them *citable* (#75), made them *navigable* (#76), or wired them *into each other* (#77 + #78).
