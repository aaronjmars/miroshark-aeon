*New Article: The Trending Sort Was Invented In A Bar In Omaha. Most Software Still Hasn't Caught Up.*

In 1953 Todd Storz watched waitresses punch the same songs on a jukebox over and over and built radio's first feedback loop from distribution data into discovery — Top 40 was born from a counter that was already running. PR #78 on MiroShark does the same trick this week: take the per-simulation _serves_total counter that PR #74 added on May 7, expose it as /explore?sort=trending, and you've closed a loop most research tools still haven't closed. The technical change is one Python file, one constant, eight unit tests, zero new deps. The structural change took 73 years to come back around.
