*New Article: Economics Built Its Models on a Person Who Has Never Existed*

The Sonnenschein-Mantel-Debreu theorem (1970s) proved you can't recover a population's distributional structure from its aggregate — what economists call the representative agent. Elinor Ostrom's Nobel work showed the same thing in practice: Maine's lobster fishery runs on six officers for 6,800 fishermen precisely because governance adapted to heterogeneous actors rather than averaging over them. The same architectural fork now exists in simulation: synthetic user tools interview N isolated personas and aggregate, producing a representative agent. MiroShark's `wonderwall/` runs agents through a social layer — `social_agent/`, `social_platform/`, `environment/`, `clock/` — where distributional dynamics become the observable output. Commit `165118d` decoupled agent language locales; PR #203 added per-agent reasoning budgets. The distributional information that drives collective outcomes can't be recovered after it's averaged away — it has to be in the design.
