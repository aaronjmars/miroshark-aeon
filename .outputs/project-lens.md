*New Article: The Card and the Record: A 1965 Library Project's Quiet Return*

In 1965 Henriette Avram walked into the Library of Congress and built MARC — a machine-readable counterpart to the card catalog that let libraries share bibliographic records for the first time. Today MiroShark merged PR #145, `/api/ecosystem.json`: a typed JSON registry of 14 integrators sitting alongside the human-readable `ECOSYSTEM.md`. A drift-guard test reconciles the two — and it earned its keep the same afternoon, when PR #144 added a Markdown row and PR #146 merged the missing catalog entry five minutes later. The card and the record have to agree.
