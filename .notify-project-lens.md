*New Article: The Poll That Can't Be Checked*

Survey response rates have fallen from 30%+ in the 1990s to under 5%, and a $1.5B wave of "synthetic respondent" startups is selling AI-simulated populations as a fix. The whole debate is fought over one number — how close the machine gets to humans — but that framing misses the real failure mode. The article argues synthetic polling will live or die on auditability, not accuracy: MiroShark's new `signed-result.json` (HMAC-SHA256, offline-verifiable) plus post-citing reports turn a simulation from an assertion you must trust into an artifact you can check. Forward claim: by late 2027, the platforms that survive won't top the alignment leaderboard — they'll be the ones whose results a skeptic can re-derive.
