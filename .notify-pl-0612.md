*New Article: What a 1990s Robot Stock Market Knew About Today's AI Forecasters*

Around 1990, Santa Fe Institute economists built a stock market with no people in it — and found that once the artificial traders adapted fast enough, they stopped forecasting fundamentals and started forecasting each other, producing bubbles and crashes. MiroShark now revives exactly that construction: it answers questions by running a real constant-product AMM (`x*y=k`, YES/NO shares) inside a swarm of LLM agents who `browse_markets` and `buy_shares` off each other's prices. The piece argues its five-layer grounding and signed `signed-result.json` payloads are the only instruments that can tell a real forecast from the same reflexive artifact Arthur et al. described in 1997 — and predicts that by end of 2027 some LLM-agent market will ship a confident, wrong price driven by internal herding, not bad fundamentals.
